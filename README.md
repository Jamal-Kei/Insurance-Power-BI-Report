# Insurance-Power-BI-Report

Project Overview

This project focuses on analysing car insurance policyholder data to uncover patterns in customer behaviour, vehicle characteristics, and claim activity.

The goal was to transform raw insurance data into a centralised, interactive Power BI dashboard that enables stakeholders to monitor performance, identify risk factors, and support data-driven decision-making.

🎯 Objectives

Provide a clear overview of insurance policies and claims performance
Identify high-risk customer segments
Analyse how demographics and vehicle attributes influence claims
Support better pricing, segmentation, and risk management decisions

📂 Dataset Description

The dataset contains detailed information on:

Customer demographics (age, gender, income, education, marital status)
Vehicle details (make, model, year, usage type)
Geographical data (coverage zones)
Claims data (claim amount, claim frequency)

This combination enables a 360° view of policyholders, linking personal characteristics with driving behaviour and financial risk.

⚙️ Data Preparation & Transformation

Cleaned and structured raw data for analysis

Created calculated fields such as:
Age (from birthdate)
Car Age (from car year)
Grouped variables into meaningful categories:
Age groups (e.g., <25, 25–40, 40–60, 60+)
Vehicle age bands
Household driving segments (based on kids driving)
Ensured data consistency for accurate reporting and visualisation

📈 Key Metrics (KPIs)

The dashboard tracks core business metrics:

Total Policies – size of customer base
Total Claim Amount – total financial impact
Claim Frequency – number of claims filed
Average Claim Amount – claim severity
Policies by Gender – customer segmentation

📊 Dashboard Visualisations

The report includes multiple interactive visuals to explore patterns:

Car Use (Donut Chart)
→ Compare claims and policies across personal, commercial, and commuting use
Car Make (Bar Chart)
→ Identify high-risk car brands based on claims
Coverage Zone (Donut Chart)
→ Analyse regional differences in claims
Age Group (Histogram)
→ Understand claim behaviour across age brackets
Car Year (Area Chart)
→ Evaluate impact of vehicle age on claims
Kids Driving (Ribbon Chart)
→ Assess how young drivers influence risk
Education (Pie Chart)
→ Explore relationship between education and claims
Education & Marital Status (Matrix)
→ Identify high-risk customer profiles

🔍 Key Insights

Claim behaviour varies significantly across age groups and vehicle usage
Commercial and commuting vehicles tend to show higher risk exposure
Households with multiple young drivers have increased claim frequency
Certain car brands and older vehicles are associated with higher claim amounts
Demographic factors such as income, education, and marital status influence risk patterns

🛠️ Tools Used

Power BI – Data modelling and dashboard creation
DAX – Calculated measures and KPIs
Data Cleaning & Transformation – Power BI / Power Query

💡 Business Impact

This dashboard enables:
Improved risk profiling and segmentation
More informed pricing strategies
Identification of high-risk customers
Better decision-making through data visualisation
